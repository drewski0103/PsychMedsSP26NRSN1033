📝 Key Mnemonics

TCA Anticholinergic Effects

"Dry, Blind, Mad, Hot, Red"
  • D Dry as a bone (dry mouth, constipation, urinary retention)
  • B Blind as a bat (blurred vision, mydriasis)
  • M Mad as a hatter (confusion, delirium)
  • H Hot as a hare (hyperthermia — no sweating)
  • R Red as a beet (flushing, vasodilation)

NMS vs. Serotonin Syndrome

NMS = "Lead Pipe" · SS = "Clonus"
  • NMS Slow onset (24–72h), lead-pipe rigidity, hyperthermia, bradyreflexia, caused by antipsychotics
  • SS Rapid onset (minutes–hours), clonus/hyperreflexia, tremor, agitation, caused by serotonergic combo
  • Both Fever + AMS → stop offending drug, supportive care

Lithium Toxicity Signs

"TREMOR → SEIZURE"
  • T Tremor (coarse, worsening)
  • R Running GI (N/V/D)
  • E Encephalopathy (confusion)
  • M Movement incoordination (ataxia)
  • O Oliguria (kidneys failing)
  • R Rigidity/seizures at high levels

EPS Types — "ADAPT"

ADAPT
  • A Akathisia (restlessness, can't stay still)
  • D Dystonia (acute muscle spasms)
  • A Akinesia (reduced movement)
  • P Pseudoparkinsonism (shuffling, tremor)
  • T Tardive Dyskinesia (late, potentially irreversible)

MAOI Danger Foods — "SMACS"

SMACS
  • S Smoked/cured meats (salami, pepperoni, bacon)
  • M Mature/aged cheeses (cheddar, brie, blue cheese)
  • A Alcohol — especially wine and tap beer
  • C Concentrated yeast extracts (Marmite, Vegemite)
  • S Soy-based fermented products (miso, soy sauce)

Serotonin Syndrome — "SHIVERS"

SHIVERS
  • S Shivering / diaphoresis
  • H Hyperthermia
  • I Increased reflexes / clonus
  • V Vital sign instability (HR, BP)
  • E Encephalopathy / agitation
  • R Restlessness / tremor
  • S Stop serotonergic agents immediately
